How I can help

Common reasons people come to see me

As Homerton's Lung Cancer Lead I have a particular focus on the rapid assessment of suspected lung cancer — and I see the full range of lung and breathing problems privately. If something is worrying you, you don't have to wait.

Subspecialty focus

Suspected or diagnosed lung cancer

As Homerton's Lung Cancer Lead, I offer rapid assessment of lung nodules, an abnormal chest X-ray or CT, or worrying symptoms — with prompt diagnostics (including bronchoscopy and EBUS-TBNA), staging and clear next steps.

Asthma

Diagnosis and management of asthma, including difficult-to-control or adult-onset asthma and specialist treatment options.

Chronic cough & breathlessness

Investigation of a persistent cough or unexplained breathlessness to find the cause and a treatment that works.

COPD & chest infections

Diagnosis and management of COPD, recurrent chest infections and breathlessness — to keep you well and out of hospital.

Obstructive sleep apnoea

Assessment of snoring, disturbed sleep and daytime sleepiness, with sleep testing and treatment such as CPAP where it's needed.

Other respiratory & second opinions

Abnormal scans, interstitial lung disease, occupational lung disease, pleural disease, and second opinions on an existing diagnosis.

Diagnostic & interventional procedures

Getting to a diagnosis, quickly

When tests are needed, I carry out the key respiratory procedures myself — so your diagnosis isn't held up waiting for another team.

Bronchoscopy

A thin camera examination of the airways to investigate symptoms, abnormal scans and infections, and to take biopsies.

EBUS-TBNA

Endobronchial ultrasound with needle sampling of lymph nodes — a key test for diagnosing and staging lung cancer accurately.

Pleural procedures

Assessment and treatment of fluid around the lung, including pleural aspiration and chest drain insertion.

About Dr Reynolds

A consultant who listens, and explains

I am a Consultant in Respiratory and Acute Medicine, and Lung Cancer Lead, at Homerton Healthcare NHS Trust. My clinical interests span lung cancer, asthma, interstitial lung disease, occupational lung disease and pleural disease, and I also advise the NHS Race and Health Observatory.

I qualified from University College London in 2008, trained in respiratory medicine in North West London, and completed a PhD in pulmonary fibrosis in 2019 at the Royal Brompton Hospital and the National Heart and Lung Institute, Imperial College London.

My approach is straightforward: take your concerns seriously, arrange the right tests without delay, and explain what's going on in plain language — so you leave with a clear understanding and a plan.
